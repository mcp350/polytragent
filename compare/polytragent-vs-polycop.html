Detailed Comparison

1. Core Philosophy: Proactive vs Reactive

Polytragent: Proactive Research

Polytragent analyzes every market independently. We don't care who's trading or how much they're winning. We care about whether the odds match reality. When we find a market where the collective belief has drifted from fundamentals, we alert you—before other traders pile in.

This is proactive trading. You find the edge, then you execute.

Example:

A political market has been trading at 35% for a candidate, but recent polling data shows 48% support. Polytragent identifies this mispricing and recommends entering before the market corrects. By the time PolyCop users see a successful wallet buying, the odds have already moved from 35% to 42%.

PolyCop: Reactive Copying

PolyCop waits for successful wallets to move first. When a chosen wallet enters a market, PolyCop instantly copies the trade. This is fast but reactive. You're always one step behind. By the time you copy, smart money has already profited.

This is reactive trading. You follow the crowd, not lead it.

Example:

The same political market is trading at 35%. A successful wallet buys at 35%, replicating their bullish view. PolyCop instantly copies at 35%, but that wallet has inside information and position-taking power that you don't. As they buy more, odds move to 38%, 42%, 45%. You entered at their average cost, but you're always buying after they've already positioned.

2. Data Analysis: Millions of Signals vs Single Metric

Polytragent analyzes multiple dimensions:

  • Trading patterns across Polymarket and Manifold Markets
  • Recent news and external events
  • On-chain data and funding flows
  • Market structure and incentive analysis
  • Crowd psychology and behavioral patterns
  • Cross-market arbitrage opportunities

PolyCop uses one metric:

  • Did a successful wallet trade? If yes, copy it.

This is why Polytragent finds edges PolyCop misses. We understand the "why" behind markets. PolyCop doesn't ask why—it just copies.

3. Profitability: Intelligence vs Wallet Selection

With PolyCop, your profitability depends entirely on which wallets you choose to copy. Choose the wrong wallet, and you lose money. Choose three wallets, and their performance variance affects your returns.

With Polytragent, your profitability depends on market analysis quality. Our AI gets smarter with every prediction. As we track accuracy and learn, our recommendations improve.

This is the fundamental difference: PolyCop's returns are limited by the wallets you can find. Polytragent's returns improve over time as our model learns.

4. Cross-Platform Opportunities

PolyCop works only on Polymarket. This limits your opportunities to one market platform.

Polytragent analyzes both Polymarket and Manifold Markets simultaneously. This reveals:

  • Arbitrage opportunities (same event, different odds across platforms)
  • A larger set of potential profitable markets
  • Cross-platform hedging strategies
  • Duplicate markets with different pricing

More platforms = more opportunities = more profitable edge.

5. Pricing: Fixed vs Variable

PolyCop: 0.5% per trade

If you make 10 trades of $500 each, you pay 0.5% × $5,000 = $25. If you make 100 trades, you pay $250. The more active you are, the more you pay. This creates a perverse incentive—you're punished for trading more.

Polytragent: $24.99 - $99/month

Whether you make 1 trade or 100 trades, you pay the same. This aligns incentives—we want you to act on every good opportunity, not just the best ones, because our fee is fixed.

For active traders, fixed pricing is dramatically cheaper.

6. Speed vs Accuracy Trade-off

PolyCop is extremely fast (sub-second), but speed comes at a cost: accuracy. You're always entering after others. Your entry point is worse than the leading wallet's entry.

Polytragent takes 2-3 seconds to analyze markets (research time), but you get a significantly better entry point because we identify opportunities before the crowd notices them. A 2-3 second delay for a better entry is a worthwhile trade-off.

Here's the math: If Polytragent finds a 10% edge and enters 5% better than PolyCop's average entry, the speed difference is irrelevant. Better analysis > faster execution.
