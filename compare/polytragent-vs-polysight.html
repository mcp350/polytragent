Polytragent vs Polysight

Updated March 2026

Both are AI-powered Polymarket tools. But they're built for different traders. Polytragent goes deep with research-grade analysis and self-improving accuracy tracking. Polysight casts a wider net with multi-platform analytics and quick metrics.

Quick Verdict

Choose Polytragent for deep research with accuracy tracking and Manifold cross-referencing. Choose Polysight for quick analytics and multi-platform views across Polymarket, Kalshi, Manifold, and Zeitgeist.

Where Polytragent Wins

Polytragent is purpose-built for traders who want to understand the "why" behind trades, not just the "what." Here's what sets it apart:

  • Deeper research capability. Claude Sonnet's reasoning engine conducts multi-step analysis across millions of data points—sub-markets, expired markets, news, and historical patterns. You get evidence-backed recommendations, not just metrics.
  • Self-improving predictions. Polytragent tracks your prediction accuracy over time and learns from its own mistakes. This means recommendations get better the longer you use it. Polysight doesn't offer this.
  • Manifold Markets cross-referencing. Not just a dual-platform tool—Polytragent uses Manifold as a research source to validate Polymarket predictions. This gives you an edge competitors miss.
  • Transparent scoring system. Every recommendation comes with a confidence score and reasoning breakdown. You know exactly why Polytragent suggests a trade.
  • Better for serious traders. If you're treating prediction markets as a skill—not a casual hobby—Polytragent's research depth and accuracy tracking will compound your edge over months and years.

Where Polysight Wins

Polysight is built for breadth, not depth. If you trade across multiple platforms, it shines:

  • Multi-platform coverage. Polysight monitors Polymarket, Kalshi, Manifold, and Zeitgeist in one dashboard. Polytragent focuses on Polymarket + Manifold. For cross-market traders, Polysight is more convenient.
  • Free tier available. Polysight offers a freemium model, so you can test it without paying. Polytragent requires a subscription from day one.
  • Quick metrics. If you want fast, snapshot-style analytics—volume, open interest, trader composition—Polysight's 30+ custom metrics dashboard is faster than digging through Polytragent's detailed research reports.
  • Mispriced market alerts. Polysight's algorithm surfaces arbitrage opportunities across platforms automatically. Useful for hunters of quick inefficiencies.
  • Lower commitment. No subscription required to start. Good for casual traders testing the waters.

Detailed Comparison: Approach & Philosophy

Polytragent: The Research-Grade AI Agent

Polytragent treats prediction markets like serious investing. It uses Claude Sonnet (Anthropic's reasoning-optimized model) to conduct deep, multi-step analysis. Each recommendation includes:

  • Evidence from multiple data sources (sub-markets, expired markets, news sentiment)
  • Confidence scoring based on model uncertainty
  • Accuracy tracking—what percentage of its recommendations have been right
  • Reasoning transparency—why it recommended a specific trade

This is designed for traders who want to build a repeatable, edge-driven strategy. Over time, you can see which of Polytragent's recommendations have performed well and calibrate your risk accordingly.

Polysight: The Multi-Platform Dashboard

Polysight is a monitor-and-alert system. It aggregates data from four platforms and surfaces opportunities in real-time. Think of it like a market radar. Instead of deep reasoning, it relies on:

  • Custom metrics (volume, open interest, trader concentration)
  • Cross-platform arbitrage detection
  • Mispriced market alerts
  • Historical pattern matching via Vertex AI

Useful for traders executing many small trades across platforms. Less useful if you want to understand the reasoning behind each trade.

The Truth: They're Not Really Competitors

Here's what most comparison articles don't tell you: Polytragent and Polysight solve different problems.

Polysight is a multi-platform monitor. Polytragent is a research engine. Many traders use both—Polysight to scan for opportunities, Polytragent to analyze them deeply before executing.

That said, if you can only choose one:

  • For edge building: Polytragent. Its accuracy tracking and reasoning transparency let you compound a skill over time.
  • For convenience: Polysight. If you trade across four platforms, you need it just to stay sane.

Accuracy & Track Record

Both platforms claim to use AI, but the proof is in the data:

Polytragent: Tracks each user's accuracy per recommendation over time. You can see what percentage of its suggestions have been correct. Publicly available accuracy metrics on product dashboard.

Polysight: No transparent accuracy tracking. The platform publishes blog posts on successful trade alerts, but there's no user-facing accuracy dashboard.

If you care about verifiable performance, Polytragent has the edge. You can audit your own results month-by-month.
