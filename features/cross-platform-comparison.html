Why This Matters

Platforms Price Markets Differently

Each prediction platform has different liquidity, forecaster pools, and market dynamics. The same event might trade at 40% on one platform and 68% on another. That gap is arbitrage waiting to be claimed.

Manually Tracking Across Platforms Wastes Hours

You'd need to: search for the same market on Manifold, compare prices, calculate divergence, repeat for dozens of markets. By then the spread has compressed. The bot does this instantly.

Other Comparison Sources Are Dead

Metaculus API is deprecated. Swift Centre hasn't updated forecasts. Manifold is the only active cross-platform comparison source. Polytragent integrates it automatically.

How It Works

1

Extract Event Title

You send a Polymarket URL. The agent extracts the event slug and parses the market title, stripping date suffixes and resolving dates.

$ Event: "Will BTC close above 100k by Feb 28?"
2

Search Manifold Markets

The bot hits Manifold's API with a normalized query. It finds matching binary markets (not AMM markets) ranked by liquidity.

$ Manifold search: Found 3 matching markets
3

Compare Prices

For each match, the agent grabs the current YES price from both platforms. Calculates divergence: |Polymarket - Manifold| / Polymarket * 100.

$ PM: 40% | Manifold: 68% | Divergence: +28%
4

Get Comparison Results

You get a formatted comparison with both prices, divergence percentage, liquidity info, and links to trade on both platforms instantly.

$ Result: 3 markets compared • 1 divergence > 20%

Frequently Asked Questions

What platforms are compared? +
Currently Polymarket and Manifold Markets. These are the two largest active binary prediction platforms with real liquidity and market depth. We're monitoring other platforms for future integration.
Why only Manifold? +
Metaculus deprecated their API. Swift Centre hasn't updated forecasts in months. Manifold is the only prediction platform with active liquidity, real-time prices, and a working public API. Polytragent compares what's actually tradeable.
How are matching markets found? +
We normalize the market title, strip date suffixes, and query Manifold's search API. Results are ranked by liquidity. Top 3-5 matches are compared. If the market exists on Manifold, we'll find it.
What counts as significant divergence? +
We flag divergences >15% by default (e.g., 40% vs 68%). However, context matters: a divergence on a 1% liquid market is noise. A divergence on a 100k volume market is real. We include liquidity context so you can decide.
