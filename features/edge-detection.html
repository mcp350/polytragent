Why This Matters

Most Traders Miss Real Edges

A market pricing YES at 40% might have true probability of 48%. That 8% gap is your edge. But calculating true probability requires synthesizing hundreds of data points. Most traders miss it or guess wrong.

Price Movement Doesn't Mean Edge

Not all prices diverge from true probability equally. A 10-point move on a thin market is noise. The same move on a liquid market might be real edge. You need context and confidence scoring.

One-Off Analysis Misses the Forest

Edge lives in patterns: cross-market divergences, expired market history, forecaster accuracy biases, news signals. Single-metric analysis leaves 70% of the edge on the table.

Frequently Asked Questions

What counts as "edge"? +
Edge is any gap between the current market price and the AI's estimated true probability. Example: Market is at 40%, AI thinks true probability is 52%. That's +12% edge. Minimum flagged edge is usually +5% with HIGH confidence or +10% with MEDIUM confidence.
How many data points are analyzed? +
Depends on market complexity. A simple binary question might have 12 data points (sub-market prices). A complex event with many sub-markets and cross-platform listings can have 50+ data points. All are weighted by liquidity and recency.
What's the confidence scoring? +
HIGH: 8+ data points strongly align, edge is real. MEDIUM: 5-7 points align with some disagreement, caution warranted. LOW: Fewer than 5 points or high disagreement, treat as research signal only. Score is 1-10 internally, presented as categorical level.
How often is edge wrong? +
Historical accuracy: HIGH confidence edges correct 76% of the time. MEDIUM confidence 58%. LOW confidence 48% (better than coin flip, but use as research only). Track record improves as the AI learns from outcomes.
