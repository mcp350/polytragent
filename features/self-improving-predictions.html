Self-Improving AI Predictions

Every prediction is logged and tracked. When markets resolve, the AI learns from the outcome. Calibration bias is corrected. Weak topics are identified. Your forecasting engine gets smarter every day.

Why This Matters

Most AI Never Learns from Mistakes

Static models make the same mistakes forever. Polytragent's AI has full visibility into its own accuracy history. It sees calibration biases (e.g., overconfident on politics by +3%) and corrects them in real-time.

No Way to Compare AI Forecasters

You can't evaluate your AI if you don't track it. Polytragent logs every prediction with reasoning, outcome, and P&L. You see exactly where your AI is strong and where it struggles.

AI Confidence Is Often Overconfident

AI tends to be too certain on topics it doesn't understand well. The prediction store tracks topic-level accuracy: politics 72%, economics 85%, crypto 60%. The AI learns these gaps and adjusts.

How It Works

1

Every Prediction Logged

When you request analysis, the bot stores: market ID, AI forecast %, recommendation (BUY_YES/BUY_NO/HOLD), Polymarket price, confidence level, and reasoning snippet in predictions.json.

$ Prediction logged: ID 12345 | Confidence: HIGH | Edge: +8%
2

Background Resolver Checks Outcomes

Every 6 hours, a scheduled task queries the Polymarket API for all unresolved predictions. When a market resolves, it records YES or NO outcome and marks the prediction correct or wrong.

$ Resolved: 3 predictions • Correct: 2 • Wrong: 1
3

Calculate Calibration Bias

The AI sees its full history: overall win rate, accuracy by topic, confidence level accuracy (HIGH predictions correct 78% vs 52% for LOW), recent performance. It identifies systematic biases.

$ Politics: Overconfident by +3.2% | Crypto: Underconfident by -1.8%
4

Adjust and Improve

The AI is explicitly instructed to learn from biases. Next prediction run, it factors in topic accuracy, recent win/loss streak, and confidence calibration. The loop continues forever.

$ Adjusting confidence on politics: HIGH → MEDIUM

Frequently Asked Questions

How does it track accuracy? +
Every prediction is stored in predictions.json with metadata: market ID, forecast %, confidence, reasoning. When markets resolve, the background resolver checks outcomes every 6 hours and marks predictions correct or wrong. This data feeds back into the AI.
What's calibration bias? +
Calibration bias is when the AI is systematically over- or under-confident. Example: If the AI says HIGH confidence 100 times but is only right 65% of the time, it's overconfident by 15%. Polytragent detects this and adjusts future forecasts.
How often does it update? +
The background resolver runs every 6 hours to check for resolved markets. Your accuracy stats update continuously as outcomes are recorded. The AI learns on the next prediction request after new data arrives.
Can I see my track record? +
Yes. Use /predict-track history to see all predictions, /predict-track stats for accuracy by topic, and /predict-track recent for your last 20 predictions with outcomes. Full transparency into AI performance.
